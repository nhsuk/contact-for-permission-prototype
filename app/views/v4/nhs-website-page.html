This is the NHS Sign up to be contacted about this service.

You can use it to let researchers know you'd like to take part in clinical trials for vaccine for coronavirus (COVID-19).

Clinical trials are studies that compare one medicine or treatment with another. They are the only way to find effective treatments and vaccines for coronavirus.

{{ warningCallout({ "heading": "Important", "HTML": "

You are not joining a clinical trial when you use the Sign up to be contacted service. You're letting researchers know you're happy for them to contact you.

" }) }}

How the Sign up tp be contacted service works

We'll ask you some questions about yourself, and then ask for your consent (permission) for someone working on a clinical trial to contact you.

You may be contacted within weeks, months or not atall. It depends how many people are needed.

Find out more about taking part in clinical trials on the Be a part of research website.

Who can use the Sign up to be contacted service

Anyone 18 or over can use this service. But it's not likely you'll be asked to take part in clinical trials if you:

  • are pregnant or breastfeeding
  • have a certain conditions, for example diabetes
  • are taking certain medicines
